# Org rollout and metrics

A target org on paper changes nothing until it is rolled out. This reference covers the phased implementation roadmap and the success metrics that tell you the change is working. Sequence change; never big-bang a reorg.

## Phased roadmap

Move from the current de-facto structure to the target Organization Model in phases. A typical shape:

1. **Pilot.** Apply the new structure to one team or business unit. Entry criteria: target model agreed, owners named. Moves: form the pilot team(s), set their interaction modes and rituals, run for a defined period. Deliverable: a working pilot plus learnings.
2. **Scale.** Roll the validated structure to the rest of the org, sequenced by dependency (a manager before their reports, a platform capability before the teams that depend on it). Deliverable: the full target structure in place.
3. **Optimize.** Tune boundaries, interaction modes, and rituals against the metrics below. Deliverable: a stabilized org and an updated Organization Model with learnings folded in.

For each phase specify: entry criteria, a checklist of concrete moves, the owner of each move, and the deliverable that closes the phase. Flag personnel-sensitive steps (role changes, departures) for the user and for legal/counsel; the skill plans the structure, it does not decide people moves.

## Communication and risk

- **Communication plan:** who is told what and when, in what order (leadership, affected teams, whole company), so the change is understood rather than feared.
- **Risks and mitigations:** the main risks (key-person flight, dependency gaps during transition, morale) each with a mitigation and an owner.

## Success metrics

Choose a few metrics that show the change is working, set a baseline before the rollout, and a target window. Useful candidates (pick what fits, do not track all):

- **Team health:** a periodic self-assessment score per team.
- **Delivery velocity:** throughput of completed work per team, period over period.
- **Cycle time:** time from start to done for a unit of work.
- **Blocking cross-team dependencies:** count of dependencies stalling work, per business unit. Lower is better.
- **Deployment / release frequency:** how often the team ships (for product/eng).
- **Discipline-community participation:** engagement in cross-cutting craft communities (a proxy for healthy horizontal alignment in a matrix).
- **Employee satisfaction / retention:** sentiment and regretted attrition.

Present metrics as a table: metric, why it matters for this change, baseline, target, and the cadence of review. Tie each back to the friction or goal the rollout is meant to fix, so the metric measures the change, not vanity.

## Living source of truth

Treat `Organization-Model.md` as a living document: update it at each phase, record status and learnings, and keep ownership and last-modified clear. The org structure (here) and the information structure (workspace-ops, which consumes the model) stay aligned as the rollout proceeds.
